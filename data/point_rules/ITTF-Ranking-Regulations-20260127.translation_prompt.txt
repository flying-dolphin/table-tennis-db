ITTF Table Tennis World Ranking Regulations 
1. BASIC PRINCIPLES 
 
1.1 The ITTF Table Tennis World Ranking is the objective merit-based method used for entries and seeding at 
ITTF and WTT events. 
1.2 The ITTF Table Tennis World Ranking is composed of the following lists: 
• Men’s Singles 
• Women’s Singles 
• Men’s Doubles Pairs 
• Women’s Doubles Pairs 
• Mixed Doubles Pairs 
• Men’s Doubles Individuals 
• Women’s Doubles Individuals 
• Mixed Doubles Individuals 
1.3 Ranking lists are published on a weekly basis on Mondays. 
1.4 Ranking lists are published for the Senior age category. 
1.5 Players and pairs are ranked when having non-expired points awarded at sanctioned events. 
1.6 Each ranking list consists of a list of players or pairs sorted by descending number of points. In case of ties, 
for presentation, entries selection and seeding, players and pairs are sorted by: 
• The player or pair who is not under ranking protection. 
• The player or pair with fewest non-expired, non-zero results. 
• If still tied, the player or pair with the non-expired result with the greatest number of points. 
• If still tied, the player or pair with the best non-expired result in WTT Grand Smash events. 
• If still tied, the player or pair with the best ranking in the previous week. 
• If still tied, the youngest player or pair (sum of the ages of the players). 
• If still tied, randomly sorted by the ranking calculation software. 
1.7 Ranking points are awarded at any sanctioned events. 
1.8 Awarded points have a validity of one (1) year,  expiring next year on the same week on which they were 
included in the ranking, except for Continental Championships, Continental Games and Continental Cups, 
which shall not expire for up to twenty-six (26) extra weeks if the number of such events counting for the 
ranking from the corresponding Continent is not of at least two (2). After expiring, events are no longer 
eligible to be included in the ranking record of a player or pair.

1.9 The ranking of a player or pair is composed of the sum of the points of the eight (8) non-expired results 
with the greatest number of points, with the following restrictions: 
1.9.1 A maximum of one (1) result from events which exclude or forbid any player(s) from a Member 
Association from being permitted to enter due to their nationality (i.e., Continental, Regional or 
similar events). 
1.9.2 A maximum of four (4) results from non-Senior events. 
1.10 Players have the right to request being removed from the rankings by submitting the retirement letter at 
https://ittf.com/rankings, signed by themselves and their Member Association, to rankings@ittf.com. 
Coming back to competition after that implies starting ranked with no ranking points.

3. POINTS 
 
3.1 The awarded points at each event for a given player consist of: 
3.1.1 Singles, Doubles and Mixed Doubles competitions: 
3.1.1.1 Points for the round reached at either the Main Draw or the Qualifying Draw, or the 
final position in the Qualifying Group for players not progressing to the Main Draw. 
3.1.1.2 Additional Qualifying points (QUAL) if the player reaches the Main Draw starting in the 
Qualifying stage. 
3.1.2 Team competitions: points for the round reached by the team to be split between the members 
(players and pairs), based on their share of wins of the total  number of matches (or games, for 
game-based competitions, e.g. ITTF Mixed Team World Cup) won by the team, with the following 
conditions: 
3.1.2.1 Requirement to achieve a minimum of 10% share to have points awarded. 
3.1.2.2 Maximum of 70% share shall be awarded to any player/pair. 
3.2 Players or pairs having a bye in the first round of the Main Draw and then losing in the next round, shall 
have points awarded for the round in which they had the bye. 
3.3 Players or pairs eliminated in the Qualifying Stage and progressing to the Main Draw as lucky-losers shall 
be awarded points for the final position reached either in the Main Draw or the Qualifying Stage, whichever 
of the two has the greatest number of points, and without any additional Qualifying points (QUAL). 
3.4 Ranking points are awarded as per the following tables, depending on the event kind, subevent and final 
position, where: 
 
• QUAL: additional Qualifying points as per point 3.1.1.2. 
• QER: Qualifying Elimination Round for events with Qualifying played in Groups and an additional 
round between the Groups and the Main Draw. 
• MD: Main Draw size. E.g. MD32 – Main Draw of 32 players. 
• Q: Qualifying Draw size. E.g. Q64 – Qualifying Draw of 64 players. 
• Gr: Qualifying played in Groups.

ITTF Table Tennis World Ranking Regulations 
4. INCIDENTS AND PENALTIES 
 
4.1 A penalty is a result of a player or pair at a sanctioned event, awarding zero (0) points, with a validity of one 
(1) year and always included in the best player or pair results as per point 1.9. Penalties shall only replace 
other non-zero results when the player or pair has reached the maximum number of results counting for 
the ranking. 
4.2 Penalties are decided by the ITTF World Ranking Manager based on the report filed by the ITTF Competition 
Manager or the WTT Event Supervisor on site, evaluating any additional medical reports or proof provided 
by the involved players and/or their respective Member Associations. The decision shall be made 
interpreting the regulation if needed, applying fairness and equality principles. 
 
4.3 Penalties may be contested by the Member Association(s) of the affected player or pair, by writing an e -
mail to rankings@ittf.com maximum fourteen (14) days after the publication of the ranking in which the 
penalty was added. 
 
The appeal shall be accepted for consideration if it fulfils the following requirements: 
 
• The Member Association(s) of the player or pair  submitted documents, such as a medical 
certificate, visa denial letter, etc. in due time. 
• The reason for the penalty was either an interpretation as unjustified of a certain situation, or and 
administrative mistake on ITTF’s side. 
• There aren’t any previous appeals for the same penalty. 
The appeal shall be evaluated by a panel of members from the ITTF World Ranking Working Group. 
Response time: max seven (7) days. The decision of this body shall not be contested to any other bodies 
within the ITTF. 
If an appeal is accepted, any changes affecting rankings shall be applied in the next rankings to be published, 
provided that the decision is made before Sunday 20h GMT, otherwise in the following rankings. The 
corrected ranking points shall be applied to the selection of entries  and/or seeding at any upcoming 
ITTF/WTT events if the qualification and/or playing system of the event allows it.  No updates shall be 
applied to previously published rankings. 
4.4 Penalties are applied on the following lists depending on the competition: 
4.4.1 Singles: Singles ranking list. 
4.4.2 Doubles / Mixed Doubles: corresponding Pairs list and Individual list for each of the players 
deemed responsible for the penalty reason. 
4.4.3 Teams: Singles or Doubles Pairs / Individuals ranking lists for the players responsible for the 
penalty reason. 
4.5 A penalty shall be wa ived for every eight (8) ITTF/WTT events the player or pair participates in after the 
incident that caused the penalty.

ITTF Table Tennis World Ranking Regulations 
5. RANKING PROTECTION 
 
5.1 Ranking protection is the right of players to keep, for a limited period, the ranking points that they had at 
the moment they requested it, which shall be used for entry and seeding at sanctioned events. 
5.2 Ranking protection shall be applied to the Singles, Doubles Individuals and Mixed Doubles Individuals 
competitions based on the corresponding ranking lists. 
5.3 Ranking protection may be requested by the Member Association of a player if the player is injured, sick, 
pregnant or under any other circumstances that prevent him/her from competing in table tennis events, 
including sanctioned and non -sanctioned events, leagues or exhibitions, for a time between 6 and 18 
months. 
5.4 The ranking protection must be requested within six (6) months after the last sanctioned event where the 
player competed, by filling out and sending the Ranking Protection form  at https://ittf.com/rankings to 
rankings@ittf.com, together with proof documents supporting the reason of the request, such as medical 
certificates. 
The request shall be evaluated by the ITTF Events department and a decision taken and communicated to 
the requesting Member Association within seven (7) days. 
If the ranking protection is approved, the Member Association of the player must communicate in advance 
of the entries deadline of the corresponding first events in which the player is back to competition  their 
intention to participate, entering the player into them in case of events without automatic entry by ranking. 
The ranking protection must be applied to the first consecutive events of the player after the protection 
comes into effect, and shall be applied to one or more of the subevents (singles, doubles mixed doubles) in 
which the player participates in. The cancellation of the entry of the player with ranking protection shall be 
discounted from the number of used protected event s if it is deemed as unjustified as per the ranking 
penalties rules from chapter 4. 
5.5 The ranking protection duration, that is, the number of events or period when the entry and seeding using 
the protected ranking is applied, is determined by the duration of the player’s absence as: 
• Between 6 and 12 months: 3 events or up to 6 months after the start date of the first sanctioned 
event where the player competes after the absence, whichever occurs first. 
• Between 12 and 18 months: 5 events or up to 9 months after the start date of the first sanctioned 
event where the player competes after the absence, whichever occurs first. 
5.6 In the case of a player who returns to competition after an absence and gets injured again having a 
protected ranking status, a freeze of the status at that moment may be requested up to two times during 
the protection. The player’s absence must be of at l east 3 months, after which the player would continue 
with the original protection and remaining months and events.

6. CONTACT AND REGULATION 
 
6.1 The ITTF Table Tennis World Ranking and all its applications are regulated by the most updated version of 
the ITTF Table Tennis World Ranking Regulations. 
6.2 The main contact medium for any ranking matters shall be the e-mail address: rankings@ittf.com 
6.3 In case of any issues or disputes related to these regulations, they shall be reviewed by the ITTF World 
Ranking Manager, who may refer them to the ITTF World Ranking Working Group.
